the esart trio berlin has come across the atlantic to take part in carnegie halls distinctive debuts series a kind of foreign exchange program that sets up two way traffic of young artists between the united states and europe a raw big boned profile and fierce musical energy propel this group sometimes in the right directions sometimes not the esart played at weill recital hall on friday night the players chose their music wisely beethovens piano trio in e flat op # no # finds the great man investing countrified almost rude musical humor with sophisticated jokes of his own altered phrase lengths rhythmic surprises and sudden harmonic transformations this is the world of haydn but with less of haydns irony and a more direct application of muscle the esart trio attacked it accordingly wolfgang rihms fremde szene ii seemed to gratify every urge and ambition of this young group forward aggressive filled with big gestures mr rihms piece sounds like schumann heard through a closed door the familiar schumanesque signatures fade in and out of tonality the composer seems to be telling us that even when his language is farthest from major and minor keys the grammar and sentence structure of his writing always refer back to them in both these pieces the esart players were not afraid to be loud indeed they appeared to enjoy it dvoraks dumky trio steered them toward their better instincts and allowed some lovely touching solo playing the musicians are nurit stark violinist hila karni cellist and cedric pescia pianist what they might try to develop is a sound of their own this need not be something sleek and uniform but rather a point at which three individual sounds can create a fourth a conjunction of personalities that preserves the current energies but observes them with a touch of detachment the daedalus quartet will appear at the next distinctive debuts concert on dec # classical music review
