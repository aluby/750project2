began a descent into obscurity along with the rest of catalanis works soon after the composers death the next year in the united states la wally has been a rarity since the last metropolitan opera staging in # but recordings pirated from italian performances and a commercial set starring renata tebaldi have made it a cult classic high on many an operaphiles list of unjustly neglected works lately la wally has been edging into the spotlight last year the sarasota opera offered the work as its seasons drawing card and on tuesday evening eve queler led the opera orchestra of new york in a concert version at carnegie hall la wally which has a libretto by luigi illica who went on to more durable collaborations with puccini is set in the swiss alps and is about wally a headstrong woman who is in love with hagenbach a hunter from the next village hagenbach and stromminger wallys father loathe each other however and when wally refuses her fathers demand that she marry gellner her fathers bailiff she leaves home hagenbach falls tentatively into her grasp later and survives gellners attempt at wallys behest to kill him in the final act he and wally finally get together but only momentarily after they declare their mutual love hagenbach is killed by an avalanche and wally leaps to her death catalanis response to this is an exquisite score that melds the lyricism of the italian vocal style with a wagnerian kind of detailed pictorial orchestral writing the returning huntsmen hagenbachs battle with a bear the swirling snowstorms and the final avalanche are all depicted vividly and the works many preludes interludes and choruses are both lovely and functional ms queler and her soloists hinted at the scores charms but came well short of realizing them fully aprile millo in the title role presented her familiar diva imitation a melodramatic posturing in the sarah bernhardt style tempered by the studied poise of gestures that look copied from ancient roman murals one could not fault her on purely vocal grounds the roles range is easily at her command and she glided through her arias with an elegant legato line and some modest embellishments adhering closely to the contours of the tebaldi recording she did not go much beneath the surface though and for all her mugging one waited in vain for her to bring some dimension to the emotionally complex role she also bobbled her big first act aria ebben ne andro lontana losing her place early on and lagging behind the orchestra elsewhere still her partisans cheered her loudly kristjan johannsson was a steely stentorian hagenbach his tenor is powerful and not unattractive but particularly in the first act he showed an unfortunate tendency to shout matteo manuguerra used his still strong baritone artfully as gellner and dawn kotoski sang walters music attractively the cast also included two young rich toned basses richard zeller as the old soldier and max wittges as stromminger ms queler drew a lovely robust choral sound from the schola cantorum but the works important orchestral underpinning was without subtlety in passages that in the score vacillate between mezzo forte and pianissimo for example ms quelers players chugged along at a steady dynamic ensemble problems plagued the performance too particularly in the preludes to the first and last movements
