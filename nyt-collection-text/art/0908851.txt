ilan averbuch nancy hoffman gallery # west broadway near prince street soho through feb # on a recent trip to india where new york city manhole covers are made ilan averbuch an israeli born sculptor discovered the virtues of cast iron and he has managed to work this solid raw structural material into several vigorous sculptural arrangements for the best of them the river he has cast in iron the goatskins used in india for carrying water they take a kind of ghostly form of the animal itself two rows of goatskins are divided by a horizontal line of heavy stone paving materials on the top row the animal forms trot along right side up on the bottom they are suspended upside down in mirror image like reflections in a river a number of long bamboo poles also cast in iron support the entire structure so that it appears to be on stilts a preindustrial primitive age is tellingly evoked and it gives you a shiver or two a very large piece air is made of more conventional materials wood stone and glass taking the form of an inflated balloon lying on its side its stone capped wooden tail saucily flipped up its made of a gridded wood framework with glass panes a playful caricature of a real hot air balloon its appealing in its scale and klutziness as a sculptor mr averbuch is not one for elegant refinements but rawness is his strength grace glueck art in review
